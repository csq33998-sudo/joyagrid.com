Color

How to build better streetwear color palettes

A good outfit search is not only about finding a strong item. It is about finding items that work with the colors you already wear.

Start with two reliable neutrals

Black, white, gray, navy, olive, cream, and washed brown are easier to repeat across outfits. Choose two base colors before adding louder pieces.

Add one accent color

A red cap, yellow hoodie, green bag, or blue sneaker can make a simple outfit feel intentional. Keep the accent controlled so it reads as a choice, not clutter.

Use washed tones for softer outfits

Washed black, faded blue, sun-bleached green, and muted burgundy pair well with denim, canvas, fleece, and vintage-inspired sneakers.

Balance contrast

If the top is bright, keep pants and shoes quieter. If the shoes are loud, let them be the visual anchor and avoid competing accessories.

Search with color words

Add color terms after the category: black cargo pants, washed hoodie, olive jacket, gray sneaker, or cream tote bag.

Try a color search

Start with a wearable neutral and refine from there.
