How to evaluate bags and accessories from photos

Small items can make a simple outfit feel finished, but they are easy to overbuy. Use photos to judge whether the piece will actually work in your daily rotation.

Check scale and proportion

A tote, shoulder bag, or crossbody can look very different depending on body size and outfit. Look for dimensions, then compare them with a bag you already own.

Inspect hardware and straps

For bags, pay attention to zipper lines, buckles, strap stitching, handle attachment, and metal finish.

Think about daily use

Ask what the item needs to carry. A small shoulder bag may look good but fail if it cannot hold your daily essentials.

Avoid filler additions

Because accessories are often cheaper than outerwear or shoes, it is tempting to add too many. Save only the ones you can imagine wearing with multiple outfits.

Search a useful accessory

Start with a practical bag type before browsing more decorative pieces.
